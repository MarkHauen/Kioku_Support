Getting Started

How do I download Kioku?

Kioku is available for free on the Apple App Store. Search for "Kioku" or visit the link on our marketing page. It works on iPhone with iOS 15+ and Apple Watch with watchOS 8+.

Do I need an account to use Kioku?

No. Kioku doesn't require an account, login, or email address. You can start adding medications and habits immediately after launching the app.

Is Kioku free?

Yes, Kioku is completely free. There are no subscriptions, in-app purchases, or hidden fees.

How do I add a medication?

Open the Medications tab, tap the + button, and follow the onboarding wizard. You'll enter the medication name, dosage, frequency, reminders, and any notes. Changes are saved automatically.

Medications & Reminders

Will Kioku remind me to take my medication?

Yes. You can set custom reminders for each medication. Reminders appear as notifications on your iPhone and Apple Watch. You can also choose "Any time" for medications where the exact time is flexible.

Can I set multiple reminders for the same medication?

Yes. You can add multiple alarms to a single medication, each with different times and days of the week.

What does PRN mean?

"PRN" (as needed) medications are ones you take when needed, not on a fixed schedule. Kioku won't send automatic reminders for PRN medications, but they're still logged when you take them.

Can I edit a medication after I've created it?

Yes. Open the medication in the list, tap "Edit," and make changes. Your dose history will be preserved.

How do I log that I've taken a dose?

On your iPhone, open the Medications tab and tap "Take dose" on the medication. On your Apple Watch, open the app and tap the dose row to expand it, then tap "Take dose."

Can I use NFC tags to log medications?

Yes. If you have NFC tags, you can link them to medications in Kioku. Tap an NFC tag with your iPhone to instantly log a dose.

Healthy Habits

How do I add a habit?

Open the Habits tab, tap the + button, and choose a habit from the curated library (like "Drink water," "Take a walk," etc.) or create a custom one. Set reminders and you're all set.

How do streaks work?

A streak is the number of consecutive days you've completed a habit. The more days in a row, the higher your streak. If you miss a day, the streak resets but you can always start a new one.

What are celebrations?

When you complete a habit, Kioku shows you a celebration with confetti and positive feedback. Special milestones (like reaching a streak of 7, 14, or 30 days) trigger even bigger celebrations!

Can I undo a habit completion?

Yes. Open the habit and tap "Undo" to remove today's completion if you logged it by mistake.

Apple Watch

Does Kioku work on Apple Watch?

Yes. Kioku has a full companion app for Apple Watch. You can view due medications, log doses, and manage habits right from your wrist.

How do I sync my data between iPhone and Apple Watch?

Syncing is automatic. Pair your Apple Watch with your iPhone in the usual way, and Kioku will sync medications and habits automatically in the background.

Can I use Kioku on Apple Watch without my iPhone?

Yes. Your Apple Watch has a copy of your medication and habit data. You can view and log doses even when your iPhone is not nearby (as long as you've synced at least once).

Will the watch app work offline?

Yes. All features work offline on both iPhone and Apple Watch.

Privacy & Security

Is my data encrypted?

Yes. All your medication and habit data is encrypted on your device using industry-standard AES-256-GCM encryption. Keys are stored securely in your device's Keychain.

Does Kioku send my data to servers?

No. By default, your data stays entirely on your own devices (iPhone and Apple Watch). Optional syncing to a personal backend is available for advanced users, but Kioku never sends data to our servers.

Can Kioku see my medications?

No. Kioku is built to keep your data on your devices. We have no access to your medications, habits, or any personal information.

Does Kioku track or analytics my usage?

No. Kioku does not use any analytics, tracking, or advertising SDKs. We don't collect usage data.

Will Kioku ever sell my data?

Absolutely not. Kioku doesn't have any business model based on data. We don't sell, share, or monetize your information in any way.

Where can I read the full privacy policy?

Our full Privacy Policy is available in the app's Settings tab and on our GitHub repository.

Troubleshooting

I'm not getting reminders. Why?

Check that:

1. Notifications are enabled for Kioku in Settings > Notifications
2. The medication/habit has a reminder set
3. The reminder time hasn't passed for today
4. Your device is not in Do Not Disturb mode

My Apple Watch isn't syncing with my iPhone.

Try these steps:

1. Make sure both devices are paired and connected
2. Force-close the Kioku app on both devices and reopen
3. Restart your Apple Watch
4. If syncing still fails, check that Bluetooth is on and both devices are in range

I accidentally deleted a medication. Can I recover it?

Once deleted, the medication is permanently removed (though your dose history remains in the app's internal log). You can always recreate the medication from scratch by tapping + on the Medications tab.

Kioku crashed or isn't working. What should I do?

Try force-closing the app and reopening it. If the problem persists, check that you're running a recent version of Kioku and your iOS/watchOS is up to date. Restart your device if needed.
